9/8: Fimbriated Moray Eel

The fimbriated moray, also known as dark-spotted moray, spot-face moray, or cheetah moray, is a moray eel of the family Muraenidae. It is a medium-sized moray which can reach a maximum length of 31 inches. Its serpentine body has a white cream to light brown background color dotted with numerous black spots which vary in size and shape depending on the individual and maturity. Its head has a tapered snout and is greenish-yellow with black dots.

The spot-face moray is widespread throughout the tropical waters of the Indo-Pacific area, mainly on the coastal reefs of oceanic islands from Madagascar to Polynesia and from south Japan to New Caledonia. Inhabits lagoons, reef flats and seaward reefs at depths around 6 - 170 feet and stays under rocks and/or in caves during daytime and hunts prey during evening hours when it feeds mainly on small fishes and crustaceans. Generally shy, it will bite if threatened
