9/22: Humboldt Squid

Measuring up to 6.5 feet long, the Humboldt squid is a real-life underwater monster. These aggressive predators are known for their lightning-fast speed, sharp beaks, and insatiable appetites. They've even been known to attack divers and small boats! But don't let their fearsome reputation fool you, Humboldt squid are also incredibly intelligent and social creatures, sometimes hunting in large coordinated packs.

Other general squid facts:

They Can Edit Their Own Brain Genes: In most animals, genetic information is decreed by DNA. Then RNA faithfully carries those edicts to the organism, which shapes the body's proteins. Most animals are the net sum of information baked into their DNA — and dictated to the rest of the body. But a 2017 study shows that squid, "may have produced a special kind of evolution based on RNA editing rather than DNA mutations and could be responsible for the complex behaviour and high intelligence seen in cephalopods, some scientists believe."

They Can Ghost On You at Any Moment: Squid can deploy an inky expulsion called a pseudomorph. The ink is designed to appear in the same shape and size of the squid. They deploy their inky doppelgangers to confuse predators and escape certain death. Appropriately, it shoots out of the creature's backside — pressed from a special ink sac and mixed with a jet of water — to create the ultimate high-tail-it maneuver.

They Are Gifted Communicators: For all the time squid spend interfacing with other citizens of the sea, those tentacles may as well be fiber-optic cables. They're constantly sending signals. Like for example, when they're looking for a mate. Or not in the mood at all. When reef squid are mating, they are able to signal to their mate that they like them effectively, and at the same time, signal to other males that they basically are aggressive and not to come at them.
